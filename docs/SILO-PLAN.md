# Driveway Gates Kent — silo plan

Live site, reconciliation and gap-fill. UK English, £ GBP; the lead form is the only
CTA. Titles are provisional — SERP/PAA-validate before writing. Canonical host uses
`www.`; location pages live at `/location/` (singular).

## Local focus (what this site is built to own)

1. **The coast and salt-corrosion spec** — marine-grade aluminium, hot-dip
   galvanising, Accoya for the Whitstable-Thanet-Folkestone belt. This is the site's
   strongest, most defensible angle ("do metal gates rust by the sea").
2. **Kent vernacular property stock** — oast houses, Wealden farmhouses, barn
   conversions; bespoke hardwood estate gates, often listed/curtilage-listed.
3. **Twelve-council fragmentation + two named AONBs** (Kent Downs, High Weald) +
   M25 Green Belt — granular, locally-true planning content.
4. **Rural off-grid power / long agricultural driveways** — solar/battery vs
   trenching down a 200m farm drive.
5. **Rural and agricultural crime** — farm/ATV/plant theft, gate-staking raids;
   anti-lift hinges, field-gate locking.
6. **West Kent commuter premium vs East Kent value** — intra-county pricing spread.

## Priority spokes — lead clusters first

**Coast (the flagship):** do metal gates rust near the sea; best timber for a
coastal gate (Accoya); how long galvanised steel lasts in the splash zone;
aluminium vs steel for seafront plots.

**Wealden property:** what gate suits an oast house or barn conversion; five-bar and
estate field gates for rural drives; are wooden gates suitable for a listed property.

**Planning:** AONB gates (Kent Downs + High Weald); Green Belt gates near the M25;
listed-building gate consent; do you need planning permission (geo-scoped to the 12
councils).

**Rural power + crime:** solar / off-grid gate power for farm drives; do gates work
in a power cut; are metal gates more secure (rural-crime context, anti-lift hinges).
